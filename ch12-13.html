Chapter 12: Models of Computation

What is the purpose of a model?

  1. The purpose of a model is to create a replica of some physical, social, scientific, or mathematical phenomena, and to use the replica to study what would likely happen in said situation.
  2. List four phenomena of a model:
    1. Captures the essence-the important properties-of the real thing
    2. Probably differs in scale from the real thing
    3. Omits some of the details of the real thing
    4. Lacks the full functionality of the real thing
  3. List four thins a computing agent should be able to do:
    1. Accept input
    2. Store information in memory and retrieve it from memory
    3. Take actions according to algorithm instructions; the choice of what action to take may depend on the present state of the computing agent, as well as on the input item presently being processed
    4. Produce output
  4. List four characteristics of an algorithm:
    1. Be well-ordered collection
    2. Consist of unambiguous and effectively computable operations
    3. Halt in a finite amount of time
    4. Produce a result

Chapter 13: Simulation & Modeling

Define these terms:

  1. scientific method - observing the behavior of a system and formulating a hypothesis that tries to understand and explain that behavior
  2. computational model - also known as a simulation model, a computational model uses mathematical equations and/or algorithmic procedures to capture the fundamental properties and behaviors of a system
  3. descrete event simulation - modeling the behavior of a system only at an explicit and finite set of times
  4. List and describe seven reasons to construct a simulation model
    1. Existence - the system might not yet exist, so it is not possible to experiment directly on the actual system. In fact, we might be using a model to help us with the construction of the system
    2. Physical realization - the system in not contructed from entities that can be represented by physical objects. For example, it may be a social system that can only be simulated on a computer
    3. Safety - it might be too dangerous to experiment on the actual system or a physical replica.
    4. Speed of contruction - it might take too much time to construct a physical model. Sometimes it is faster to design and build a computer simulation.
    5. Time scale - some physical systems change too slowly or too quickly.
    6. Ethical behavior - some physical models have serious moral and thical consequences, perhaps the best known being the use of animals in medical research. In this case, a computational model could eliminate a great deal of suffering.
    7. Ease of modification - if we are not happy with the design of a physical model, we neet to construct a brand-new one. In a simulation, we only need to change some numerical parameters and rerun the existing model.

Illustris: "The Mother of All Computations"

What is Illustris?
A model created by a research group that claims to be the most detailed and realistic computer simulation ever built to study the formation of the universe, starting with the Big Bang, and leading up to current time.
What fields of science were involved in the Illustris computational model?
The Illustris computional model uses physics, astronomy, hydrogynamics, chemistry, and mathematics.
What does Illustris simulate?
It simulates the expansion of galaxies, the gravitational pull of matter, the formation of stars and black holes, and the motions of cosmic gases.
How long did the project take?
It took five years of development, including three months of nonstop parallel computing.
How many years of gallactic evolution are simulated in Illustris?
Illustris simulates 14 billion years of the galaxy.
Illustris was created using two supercomputers. What is a supercomputer?
A supercompter is a specialized computer, which involves having thousands of processors, in order to make billions, trillions, or even sextillions of operations.
